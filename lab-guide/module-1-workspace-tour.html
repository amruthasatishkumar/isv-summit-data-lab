Workspace Tour & ISV Architecture

Before we build, let's understand what you're building. This is a guided tour of the UrbanPulse architecture, the Fabric workspace layout you'll use today, and the ISV multi-tenancy patterns this design embodies.

Scenario - The UrbanPulse ISV

Imagine you work for UrbanPulse Analytics, an ISV that sells a real-time Smart City Operations Platform to municipalities. The City of Metropolis just signed a contract. Their three operational pain points:

🏥 HEALTH

Northwind Health Network

15 active patients streaming vitals every 8–15 seconds. Charge nurses need to know who's critical right now, not from yesterday's report.

🚆 TRANSIT

Metropolis Metra (3 lines)

Red, Blue, and Green lines reporting GPS, speed, and on-time status. Operations needs to spot delays before riders complain.

💡
The ISV play. Two different customer domains (hospital and transit) each generating real-time telemetry. UrbanPulse's value proposition isn't "we'll build you a dashboard" - it's "we'll let your operators ask questions in plain English and get answers grounded in live data, no matter which system it lives in." That's the platform you're building today.

Walkthrough - Create & Tour Your Workspace

  1. Create your Fabric workspace

    From the Fabric portal, click Workspaces in the left rail → + New workspace.

    Fill in the panel:

    • Name: urbanpulse-{your-id} (use the ID on your seat slip)
    • Description (optional): "ISV Summit Day 2 lab - UrbanPulse smart-city build"
    • Domain (optional): leave blank

    Expand Advanced at the bottom of the panel:

    • License mode: Fabric capacity
    • Capacity: fc-isvsummit-{room} (the only one you'll see)

    Click Apply. Fabric drops you into your brand-new (empty) workspace.

  3. Open the OneLake explorer (preview)

    Click the OneLake icon in the left rail. You'll see your tenant tree with your new workspace listed but no items beneath it yet. That's normal.

    You'll come back to this view in Module 2 once your Lakehouse exists - the Files/ and Tables/ folders show up under the lakehouse.

  4. Inspect workspace settings & capacity

    Back on your workspace home, click Workspace settings (gear icon, top-right). Confirm:

    • License mode: Fabric capacity (F-SKU)
    • Capacity name: fc-isvsummit-{room}

    If anything else is selected, switch it now - several items in later modules require Fabric capacity (not Trial / not Pro).

  5. Inspect access (RBAC)

    Click Manage access. As the workspace creator you're Admin. You can leave the access list as-is for the lab; in production you'd add the customer's users as Members or Contributors here.

    ⓘ
    ISV multi-tenant pattern: One workspace per customer tenant, with the ISV's automation principal as Admin and the customer's users as Members or Contributors. The optional Module 9 stretch shows how this scales across customer SKUs.

ISV Multi-Tenancy Patterns (preview)

The architecture you'll build today is what we'd actually deploy for a single customer. For a real ISV product, you'd template it. Three common Fabric multi-tenancy patterns:

Pattern How it looks Pros Cons
Workspace-per-tenant
(today's lab)		 One workspace + one capacity per customer. Hard data isolation, simple billing per tenant. More items to manage; capacity not amortized.
Workspace-per-tenant, shared capacity Many workspaces all bound to one F-SKU. Cost-efficient for many small tenants. Workspaces are still RBAC-isolated. Noisy-neighbor risk; needs careful capacity sizing.
Shared workspace, row-level isolation One workspace, one Lakehouse, tenant ID column on every table + RLS. Cheapest at scale; simple operationally. Weakest isolation; one bad query = all tenants pay.
✓
Coach tip: For your first 10 customers, start with workspace-per-tenant on a shared capacity. It's the easiest to operate and the easiest to migrate from.
